Summaries of the 7 Articles of the Constitution

Article I: Establishes the Legislative Branch (Congress – House and Senate)

Article II: Establishes the Executive Branch (President, VP, etc.)

Article III: Establishes the Judicial Branch (Supreme Court and lower courts)

Article IV: States’ powers and relations (Full Faith and Credit Clause, etc.)

Article V: Amendment process

Article VI: Supremacy Clause (Federal law > State law)

Article VII: Ratification of the Constitution

Significant Amendments to Know

1st: Freedom of speech, religion, press, assembly, petition

2nd: Right to bear arms

4th: Protection against unreasonable searches and seizures

5th: Due process, double jeopardy, self-incrimination

6th: Right to a speedy, public trial and lawyer

8th: No cruel or unusual punishment

10th: Powers not given to federal govt go to states

13th: Abolished slavery

14th: Citizenship and equal protection under the law

15th: Voting rights regardless of race

17th: Direct election of senators

19th: Women's suffrage

24th: No poll tax

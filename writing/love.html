What does it mean to love?

April 2026 · English 90

What does it mean to love? Is it that sweet feeling that stops at the right spot, not cloying nor saccharine? What about the dead squirrel on the ground the other day, run over by an unattentive driver only for crows to gnaw at its flesh.

Is this love? Love for the driver that provided food to eat, or love for the squirrel that is the food? It is certainly sweet, after all, we need to eat to survive. Maybe love is a series of actions showing one cares for another? But Barry has ridden that dusty old bike since he was five, having the money to replace it but consistently declining. One day it sits out in the rain, dilapidation seemingly creeping from out of nowhere. But Barry saves it with a brand new blue tarp, which certainly cost more than the rusty old bike. So does he not love the bike?

Is it just a strong feeling of liking someone? Enough tiramisu from that one Michelin star restaurant makes the enjoyment akin to eating just another bag of soggy gummy worms. Can gummy worms even be soggy? I read somewhere that Gelatin-based gummies weep excess water when drying, is this what it means to love? When that saccharine taste is overpowering, does it cause one to weep? Maybe so, because Barry weeped when he had to abandon his bike after it broke down yet another time. And when she broke up with her loved one, even after knowing it would end that way for the last two weeks, she cried. Her tears ran down her cheeks and her mind was blank, despite that upcoming exam. Just like a soggy gummy worm.

The Postman

April 2026 · English 90

The ground was coarse and angry and threw tiny pebbles at the postman's feet, which were equally as angry but definitely more sweaty. His feet follow his legs without protest. I watched the postman from afar, my butt glued to the bench in front of my school. Newmont high school. The postman looked at me for a split second, but for some reason he didn't have eyes. Or a mouth. Just blank skin staring. He looks away and I'm sure I'm hallucinating something. Envisioning him isn't hard. Smooth off-white flesh origami pressed over a scalp. The skull yearned for freedom and pressed back against that blank flesh.

The uniform wasn't odd. Yesterday when it had been surrounded by its kin it felt a bit sad. It longed to be special. Longed to have something to distinguish. I can't imagine what the man was thinking. Only I knew that my own coat didn't have that same longing to be special. But today, for some reason, I felt like it would achieve that regardless. The postman looked at me again.

His eyes crawled back, desperately, into his face so that he wasn't a wall of flesh anymore. I didn't see this, but only saw the empathetic, the scared, the happy, the furious eyes. And a mouth that was both a frown and a grin at the same time. His tongue whipped around his mouth, lashing against the cruelty of its jailer.

I stood up. A face, albeit one that reigned in me feelings of wanting to scream and run, was more comforting than a face with no eyes. But now instead of a tongue, something else was there. A black butterfly. No, an egg. It grabbed the air using invisible arms and propelled itself faster and faster towards me. My eyes, in a reaction they couldn't control, widened and left behind my body in an attempt to run from the man that was attempting to consume me.

That day my uniform became special. It was originally bright teal and blended in quite nicely. It told me once that it loved its color, and the unity it received from blending in with its surroundings. My eyes, ever fearful, could see myself. I am alone on the ground. My face was akin to the mailman's but simply without eyes. Eye sockets stared back at me. I assumed that it would be shadowed, but I was wrong. Instead they left a gaping hole into the center of the world.

My uniform was disappointed in me. It had told me its wish, but now it was no longer special.

And other people soon joined me. A collection of eyeballs and stared in horror upon their own bodies. Were we stuck here forever? The mailman's evil mouth continued its hunt. But my eyes were still shocked as to why his tongue wasn't pink anymore.

I also died alone

April 2026 · English 90

I also died alone. Normally there would be this feeling of disappointment that I wasn't able to do much with my life. But would I have anyways? My life was a joke yes, but that is to be expected. I didn't see it but I'm sure some of the people that feigned friendship couldn't care less about me being dead.

I guess in some way I had that person that watched me throughout the last years of my life and it makes me grateful that I clung on when times were tough. She didn't see my death, of course. And it would be selfish of me to wish that she witnessed that, because aren't you supposed to want to protect the ones we love? Yeah, I died alone. But I shouldn't wish upon anyone to have to witness some hopeless attempt at another mouthful of air.

I wasn't even riding my bike or scooter because my idiotic self let it be stolen, along with my sanity during times when classes and my emotional life was too hard. So maybe that does make me more of a joke than those that die crossing a road. Cus even crossing the road was hard for me.

Before I died I couldn't imagine dying. It always feels so far removed. Before when I thought of dying I thought of me crying in a car hearing about an old relative, or holding my rabbit after she got mauled. By that ugly dog we were babysitting because my grandpa had cancer. I feel happy that my mom's boyfriend abandoned it on a random street and it ran away. Did that mean it hated us? Do I even care?

Should I feel immoral for this? I tell myself that had I lived longer it's possible that I could have hurt the world. But perhaps I am wrong, because only those that are talented or lucky enough to make a difference are able to actually impose upon the world some level of harm.

I'm glad I died alone. And I tell myself I'm glad I died young. But I am not. I am jealous of what I could have been, whether it be to harm or to help the world. And I can't stand the thought of her eventually forgetting about me.

Problem 12

\(\text{Let }S\text{ and }T\text{ be nonempty sets of real numbers such that every real number is in }S\text{ or }T\)

\(\text{and if }s\in{S}\text{ and }t\in{T},\text{ then }s<{t}.\text{ Prove that there is a unique real number }\beta\text{ such that }\)

\(\text{every real number less than }\beta\text{ is in }S\text{ and every real number greater than }\beta\text{ is in }T.\)

\(\text{A decomposition of the reals into two sets that have these properties is called a }\textit{Dedekind cut}\text{. This is called }\textit{Dedekind's theorem.}\)

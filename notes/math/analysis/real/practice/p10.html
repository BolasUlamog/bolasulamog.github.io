Problem 10

\(\text{Show }\sqrt{n}\text{ is irrational if }n\text{ is prime.}\)

Reveal Answer

\(\text{Let }\sqrt{n}=\frac{p}{q}\text{ where }p,q\in\mathbb{Z}\text{ and }\gcd(p,q)=1\text{ (def rational number)}\)

\(\text{We can also assume }n>2\text{ since we showed the subcase of }n=2 \text{ earlier}\)

\(\text{Letting }n=\frac{p^2}{q^2}\implies{n}q^2=p^2\)

\(\text{ We have to try to show that if }n\text{ is prime, then there is no }p^2\text{ or }q^2\text{ such that }nq^2=p^2\)

\(\text{Let }p=p_1^{a_1}p_2^{a_2}...p_k^{a_k}\text{ and }q=q_1^{b_1}q_2^{b_2}...q_k^{b_k}\implies{p^2=p_1^{2a_1}p_2^{2a_2}...p_k^{2a_k}}\text{ and }q^2=q_1^{2b_1}q_2^{2b_2}...q_r^{2b_r}\text{, where }p_n,q_n\text{ are distinct prime factors}\)

\(\text{Notice that }p^2\text{ and }q^2\text{ have an even number of distinct prime factors. }\)

\(\text{Since }n{q^2}\text{ has an odd number of prime factors (Since }n\text{ is prime)}\implies{{n}q^2\not=p^2}\text{, which is a contradiction}\)

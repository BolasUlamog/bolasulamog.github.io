Math 61 - Week 6

Q: How to compute \((Df)(a)\)?

Here we will give an example: \(f:\mathbb{R}^2\to\mathbb{R}^3\), \(f=(x_1x_2^2, \sin{x_2},e^{x_1}x_2)\)

Def Directional Derivative

\(a\in{U},v\in\mathbb{R}^n\). Define the directional derivative of \(f\) at a direction \(v\) by \[(D_vf)(a)=\lim_{k\to0}\frac{f(a+kv)-f(a)}{k}=\left(\lim_{k\to0}\frac{f_1(a+kv)-f_1(a)}{k},...,\lim_{k\to0}\frac{f_m(a+kv)-f_m(a)}{k}\right)\]

Note that this is the same as the derivative of 1 variable function \(g(t)=f(a+tv)\) at \(t=0\).

Additionally, in the case \(v=e_j=(0,0,...,1,0,...,0)\in\mathbb{R}^n\), the directional derivative is called a partial derivative denoted \((D_jf)(a)\) or \(\frac{\partial{f}}{\partial{x_j}}(a)\)

Theorem:

Suppose \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\in{U}\). Then for any \(v\in\mathbb{R}^n\), the directional derivative \((D_vf)(a)\) exists and is given by \((D_vf)(a)\) exists and is given by \(\underbrace{(D_vf)(a)}_{\mathbb{R}^m}=\underbrace{[(Df)(a)]}_{\mathbb{R}^{m\times{n}}}\underbrace{v}_{\mathbb{R}^n}\)

Proof (click to expand)

Let \(\varepsilon>0\). We WTS there exists \(\delta\) s.t. \(\|\frac{f(a+kv)-f(a)}{k}-(Df)(a)v\|\) if \(0<|k|<\delta\)

If \(v=0\), then this is obvious. Assume now that \(v\neq{0}\).

By definition of \((Df)(a)\), there exists \(\delta'>0\) s.t. \[\frac{\|f(a+h)-f(a)-(Df)(a)h\|}{\|h\|}<\frac{\varepsilon}{\|v\|}\text{ whenever }\|h\|<\delta'\]

Let \(\delta=\frac{\delta'}{\|v\|}\). Suppose \(0<|k|<\delta\). Then \(h=kv\) satisfies \(0<\|h\|\|k\|<\delta\|v\|<\delta'\)

Hence \[\frac{\|f(a+kv)-f(a)-(Df)(a)kv\|}{\|k\|\|v\|}<\frac{\varepsilon}{\|v\|}\implies\|\frac{f(a+kv)-f(a)}{k}-(Df)(a)v\|<\varepsilon\]

Cor:

Assume \(f\) differentiable at \(a\)

  • 1. \((D_jf)(a)=(Df)(a)e_j=\) jth column of \((Df)(a)\)
  • 2. Suppose \(v=\sum_{j=1}^nv_je_j\). Then \((D_vf)(a)=\sum_{j=1}^n{v_j}(D_jf)(a)\)

Theorem:

Given \(U\subseteq{\mathbb{R}^n}\) open, \(f:U\to\mathbb{R}^m\)

Suppose there exists \(a\in{U}\) and \(\rho>0\), \(D_jf\) exists everywhere in \(B_\rho(a)\) for all \(j\) and assume \(D_j(f)\) is continuous at \(a\). Then \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\).

Proof (click to expand)

Let \(\varepsilon>0\). We WTS that \(\frac{\|f(a+h)-f(a)-\sum_{j=1}^n(D_jf)(a)h_j\|}{\|h\|}<\varepsilon\) whenever \(0<\|h\|<\delta\). Here \(h=\sum_{j=1}^nh_je_j\)

Define \(h^{(k)}=\sum_{j=1}^kh_je_j,k=1,...,n\)

\[f(a+h)-f(a)=f(a+h^{(n)})-f(a+h^{(n-1)})+f(a+h^{(n-1)})\] \[-f(a+h^{(n-2)})+...+f(a+h^{(1)})-f(a)\]

Now \(f(a+h^{(k)})-f(a+h^{(k-1)})=f(a+h^{(k-1)}+h_ke_k)-f(a+h^{(k-1)})\)

\(=h_k(D_kf)(a+h^{(k-1)}+\theta_kh_ke_k)\) for some \(\theta\in(0,1)\) by MVT

Hence \[\frac{\|f(a+h)-f(a)-\sum_{j=1}^n(D_jf)(a)h_j\|}{\|h\|}\]

\[=\frac{\|\sum_{j=1}^n[h_j(D_jf)(a+h^{(j-1)}+\theta_jh_je_j)]-(D_jf)(a)\|}{\|h\|}\]

\[\leq\frac{\|h\|\sum_{j=1}^n\|(D_jf)(a+h^{(j-1)}+\theta_jh_je_j)-(D_jf)(a)\|}{\|h\|}\]

\[=\sum_{j=1}^n\|(D_jf)(a+h^{(j-1)}+\theta_jh_je_j)-D_jf(a)\|\]

Since \(D_jf\) is continuous at \(a\), there exists \(\delta_j\in(0,\rho)\) s.t. \(\|D_jf(x)-(D_jf)(a)\|<\frac{\varepsilon}{n}\) whenever \(\|x-a\|<\delta\). Then \(\|h^{(j-1)}-\theta_jh_je_j\|<\delta\) for all \(j\).

Hence, from the calculations above,

\[\frac{\|f(a+h)-f(a)-\sum_{j=1}^n(D_jf)(a)h_j\|}{\|h\|}<\sum_{j=1}^n\frac{\varepsilon}{n}=\varepsilon\]

Theorem: Chain Rule

Assume \(U\subseteq\mathbb{R}^n,V\subseteq\mathbb{R}^m\) open. \(f:U\to{V},g:V\to\mathbb{R}^\ell\)

Suppose \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\in{U}\) and \(g\) is differentiable at \(f(a)\in{V}\). Then \(g\circ{f}:U\to\mathbb{R}^\ell\) is differentiable at \(a\). Morover,

\[\underbrace{D(g\circ{f})(a)}_{\mathbb{R}^{\ell\times{n}}}=\underbrace{[Dg(f(a))]}_{\mathbb{R}^{\ell\times{m}}}\underbrace{[Df(a)]}_{\mathbb{R}^{m\times{n}}}\]

Proof (click to expand)

Let \(\varepsilon>0\)

We WTS there exists \(\delta>0\) s.t. \(B_\delta(a)\subseteq{U}\) and \(\|(g\circ{f})(a+h)-(g\circ{f})(a)-[Dg(f(a))][Df(a)]h\|<\varepsilon\|h\|\) whenever \(\|h\|<\delta\)

Let \(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2\) be numbers depending on \(\varepsilon\) to be chosen later

For any given \(\varepsilon_1>0\), there exists \(\delta_1>0\) s.t. \(B_{\delta_1}(a)\subseteq{U}\) and \(\|f(a+h)-f(a)-(Df)(a)h\|<\varepsilon_1\|h\|\) whenever \(\|h\|<\delta_1\)

Additionally, for any given \(\varepsilon_2>0\) there exists \(\delta_2>0\) s.t. \(B_{\delta_2}(f(a))\subseteq{V}\) and \(\|g(y)-g(f(a))-(Dg)(f(a))(y-f(a))\|<\varepsilon_2\|y-f(a)\|\) whenever \(\|y-f(a)\|<\varepsilon_2\)

Since \(f\) is differentiable at \(a\) it is continuous at \(a\) and then there exists \(\delta_3>0\) s.t. \(\|f(a+h)-f(a)\|<\delta_2\) whenever \(h<\delta_3\), letting \(y=f(a+h)\)

For fixed \(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2>0\), take \(\delta_1,\delta_2,\delta_3\) as above. Now suppose \(\|h\|<\min\{\delta_1,\delta_3\}\)

Then \[\|(g\circ{f})(a+h)-(g\circ{f})(a)-[(Dg)(f(a))][Df(a)]h\|\leq\|g(f(a+h))-g(f(a))-(Dg)(f(a))(f(a+h)-f(a))\|+\|Dg(f(a))...\|\text{(triangle)}\]

\[\leq\varepsilon_2\|f(a+h)-f(a)\|+\|Dg(f(a))\|\|f(a+h)-f(a)-Df(a)h\|\]

\[<\varepsilon_2\|f(a+h)-f(a)\|+\|Dg(f(a))\|\varepsilon_1\|h\|\]

\[\leq\varepsilon_2(\|f(a+h)-f(a)-Df(a)h\|+\|Df(a)h\|+\|Dg(f(a))\varepsilon_1\|)\]

\[<\varepsilon_1\varepsilon_2\|h\|+\varepsilon_2\|Df(a)h\|+\|Dg(f(a))\|\varepsilon_1\|h\|\]

\[\leq(\varepsilon_1\varepsilon_2+\varepsilon_2\|Df(a)\|+\varepsilon_1\|Dg(a)\|\|f(a)\|)\|h\|\]

Now choose \(\varepsilon_1,\varepsilon_2\) s.t. the quantity above is less than epsilon and then choose \(\delta\leq\frac{1}{2}\min\{\delta_1,\delta_2\}\)

Cor:

\(f,g\) as above. Then \(D_i(g\circ{f})(a)=\sum_{k=1}^m(D_kg_i)(f(a))(D_if_k)(a)\)

Cor: 1D chain rule

If \(n=m=1\), \((g\circ{f})'(a)=g'(f(a))f'(a)\)

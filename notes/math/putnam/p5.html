1999 A-5

Prove that there is a constant \(C\) such that, if \(p(x)\) is a

polynomial of degree 1999, then

\[|p(0)|\leq{C}\int_{-1}^1{|p(x)|}\,dx\]

Click to reveal the answer

\(\text{Let }\mathcal{P}\text{ be the set of polynomials with degree 1999 or less. Thus }\mathcal{P}\text{ is a vector space with dimension 2000.}\)

\(\text{We can introduce the following norms on }\mathcal{P}: \text{ (The first one is shown you can do the second one yourself)}\)

\[\|p\|_1 = \sum_{k=0}^{1999} |p(k)|,\,\, \|p\|_2 = \int_{-1}^1 |p(x)|\, dx.\]

\(\text{Norm 1: }\)

\(\text{Positivity: }\)

\[1. \text{ Since }|p(k)|>0\text{ for all }k,\sum_{k=0}^{1999}|p(k)|>0\]

\[2. \text{ If }p=0,p(k)=0\text{ for all }k\implies\|p\|_1=0\]

\[\text{Homogeneity: }\]

\[\|\alpha{p}\|_1=\sum_{k=0}^{1999}|\alpha{p(k)}|=|\alpha|\sum_{k=0}^{1999}|{p(k)}|=|\alpha|\cdot\|p\|_1\]

\[\text{Triangle: }\]

\[\|p+q\|_1=\sum_{k=0}^{1999}{|p(k)+q(k)|}\leq\sum_{k=0}^{1999}{|p(k)|+|q(k)|}=\sum_{k=0}^{1999}{|p(k)|}+\sum_{k=0}^{1999}{|q(k)|}=\|p\|_1+\|q\|_2\]

\[\text{Since 2000 points are evaluated, that means there is only ONE zero polynomial!}\]

\[\text{Since any two norms on a finite-dimensional vector space are equivalent, there exists some }C\text{ such that }\]

\[\|p\|_1\leq{C}\|p\|_2\text{ for all }p\in\mathcal{P}\text{ and }\|p\|_1=|p(0)|+\sum_{k=1}^{1999}|p(k)|\implies{\|p\|_1}\geq|p(0)|\]

\[\text{So }|p(0)|\leq\|p\|_1\leq{C}\|p\|_2=C\int_{-1}^1{|p(x)|}\,dx\implies|p(0)|\leq{C}\int_{-1}^1{|p(x)|}\,dx\]

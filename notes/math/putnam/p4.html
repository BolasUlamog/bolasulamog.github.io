\(\text{Find polynomials }f(x),g(x),\text{ and }h(x),\text{ if they exist, such }\)

\(\text{that for all }x,\)

\[ |f(x)|-|g(x)|+h(x)=\Biggl\{ \begin{align*} -1 \qquad&\text{if }x<-1 \\ 3x+2 \:&\text{if}-1\leq{x}\leq0 \\ -2x+2 \:&\text{if }x>0 \\ \end{align*} \]

Click to reveal the answer

\(\text{Since each part of the piece wise is linear, we can first try to assume that }f(x),g(x),h(x)\text{ are all linear functions. }\)

\(\text{First we can simply denote }\mathcal{R}\text{ as the current "state" of the piecewise: }\mathcal{R}_1(x)=-1,\mathcal{R}_2(x)=3x+2,\text{etc.}\)

\(\text{Since }\mathcal{R}_2-\mathcal{R}_1=3x+3,\text{ there is a "jump" of }3x+3\)

\(\text{Notice how the function }|x|-2\text{ has a jump from }-x\text{ to }x,\text{ or a jump of }2x\)

\[\text{This means }f(x)=\frac{|\Delta\mathcal{R}|}{2}=\frac{3x+3}{2}\text{ (not }g(x)\text{ since the change was positive)}\]

\[\text{We can apply the similar logic for }g(x): \mathcal{R}_3-\mathcal{R}_2=-5x\implies{g(x)}=\frac{5x}{2}\]

\[ \text{So far...} \Biggl\lvert\frac{3x+3}{2}\Biggr\lvert-\Biggl\lvert\frac{5x}{2}\Biggr\lvert+h(x)=\Biggl\{ \begin{align*} -1 \qquad&\text{if }x<-1 \\ 3x+2 \:&\text{if}-1\leq{x}\leq0 \\ -2x+2 \:&\text{if }x>0 \\ \end{align*} \]

\[\text{Now we find an }h(x)\text{ such that }h(x)=-1-|f(x)|+|g(x)|\text{ (when }x<-1\text{)}\]

\[\implies{h(x)}=-1-\frac{5x}{2}+\frac{3x+3}{2}=-x+\frac{1}{2}\]

\[\implies\Biggl\lvert\frac{3x+3}{2}\Biggr\lvert-\Biggl\lvert\frac{5x}{2}\Biggr\lvert-x+\frac{1}{2}=\Biggl\{ \begin{align*} -1 \qquad&\text{if }x<-1 \\ 3x+2 \:&\text{if}-1\leq{x}\leq0 \\ -2x+2 \:&\text{if }x>0 \\ \end{align*} \]

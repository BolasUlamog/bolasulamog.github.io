1995 A-2

For what pairs \((a,b)\) of positive real numbers does the improper integral

\[\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\sqrt{x+a}-\sqrt{x}}-\sqrt{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{x-b}}\Biggr)dx\] converge?

Click to reveal the answer

\(\text{We can first attempt to simplify the integrand, since }\sqrt{x+a}-\sqrt{x}=\frac{a}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt{x+a}}\)

\[\text{Using this we eventually have }\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\sqrt{x+a}-\sqrt{x}}-\sqrt{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{x-b}}\Biggr)dx=\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\frac{a}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt{x+a}}}-\sqrt{\frac{b}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt{x-b}}}\Biggr)dx\]

\[=\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\frac{a}{\sqrt{x^2+ax}}}-\sqrt{\frac{b}{\sqrt{x^2-bx}}}\Biggr)dx=\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt[4]{x^2+ax}}-\frac{\sqrt{b}}{\sqrt[4]{x^2-bx}}\Biggr)dx=\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt[4]{1+\frac{a}{x}}}-\frac{\sqrt{b}}{\sqrt{x}\sqrt[4]{1-\frac{b}{x}}}\Biggr)dx\]

\[=\int_b^\infty\frac{1}{x^{1/2}}\Biggl(\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt[4]{1+\frac{a}{x}}}-\frac{\sqrt{b}}{\sqrt[4]{1-\frac{b}{x}}}\Biggr)dx\]

\[\text{Now consider the taylor series expansion of }(1+t)^{\alpha}\]

\[f(t)=(1+t)^{\alpha},f(0)=1; f'(t)=\alpha(1+t)^{\alpha-1},f'(0)=\alpha,...\]

\[\text{Since when }|t|\ll{1}\text{ (as }x \text{ approaches }\infty\text{)},(1+\frac{a}{x})^{-1/4}\approx1+\alpha{t}=1-\frac{a}{4x}\text{ and }(1-\frac{b}{x})^{-1/4}\approx1+\frac{b}{4x}\]

\[\implies\int_b^\infty\frac{1}{x^{1/2}}\Biggl(\frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt[4]{1+\frac{a}{x}}}-\frac{\sqrt{b}}{\sqrt[4]{1-\frac{b}{x}}}\Biggr)dx\approx\int_b^\infty\frac{1}{x^{1/2}}\Biggl(\sqrt{a}\Bigl(1-\frac{a}{4x}\Bigr)-\sqrt{b}\Bigl(1+\frac{b}{4x}\Bigr)\Biggr)dx\]

\[\text{Note }\sqrt{a}\Bigl(1-\frac{a}{4x}\Bigr)-\sqrt{b}\Bigl(1+\frac{b}{4x}\Bigr)=\Bigl(\sqrt{a}-\frac{a^{3/2}}{4x}\Bigr)-\Bigl(\sqrt{b}+\frac{b^{3/2}}{4x}\Bigr)=\sqrt{a}-\sqrt{b}-\frac{a^{3/2}+b^{3/2}}{4x}\]

\[\implies\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\sqrt{x+a}-\sqrt{x}}-\sqrt{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{x-b}}\Biggr)dx\approx\int_b^\infty\frac{\sqrt{a}-\sqrt{b}}{x^{1/2}}-\frac{a^{3/2}+b^{3/2}}{4x^{3/2}}+...dx\]

\[\text{ (Notice that any power after that, if you do the taylor expansion farther is in the form }\frac{p(a,b)}{x^n},\text{ where }n\geq2\text{, which converges)}\]

\[\text{So in order for }\int_b^\infty\Biggl(\sqrt{\sqrt{x+a}-\sqrt{x}}-\sqrt{\sqrt{x}-\sqrt{x-b}}\Biggr)dx\text{ to converge, }\sqrt{a}=\sqrt{b}\implies{a}=b\]

\(\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\blacksquare\)

Can an arc of a parabola inside a circle of radius 1 have

a length greater than 4?

Click to reveal the answer

\[\text{First, we know that our parabola, from now on }p(x)=ax^2+bx+c\text{, intersects the unit circle when }x^2+(ax^2+bx+c)^2=0\]

\[\text{We also know that }L=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\sqrt{1+\biggl(\frac{dp}{dx}\biggr)^2}\,dx=\int_{x_1}^{x_2}\sqrt{1+(2ax+b)^2}\,dx\]

\[\text{We can parametrize this also: }x=t, y=at^2+bt+c\]

\[\text{We can also assume that }b=0\text{ so that the parabola is centered on the y-axis. }\]

\[\text{Some theoretical parabola that follows the bottom half of the circle would have the arc length of }\pi.\]

\[\text{We want a parabola that starts at the bottom of the circle and goes above hte middle part of the circle. (something like this): }\]

\[\text{In the case where }f(x)=2x^2-1,x_1=-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}, x_2=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\]

\[\text{So }L=\int_{-\sqrt{3}/{2}}^{\sqrt{3}/{2}}\sqrt{1+16x^2}\,dx\]

\[\text{Of course we can use trig sub for the above integral: }\tan\theta=\frac{1}{4}x,\sec^2{\theta}\,d\theta=\frac{1}{4}dx\]

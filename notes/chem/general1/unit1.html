Classifications of matter

What is matter?

- Anything that has mass and volume

- Made of atoms

Can be solids, liquids, and gas

Solids

Definite shape and volume

Liquids

Definite volume

Gases

Neither definite state nor volume

How else can we classify matter?

Elements

Singular element

  • \(\ce{Na}\)
  • \(\ce{N2,O2,F2}\)
  • \(\ce{Fe,Ag,Au}\)

Compounds

Two or more different types of elements bonded together

  • \(\ce{H2O}\)
  • \(\ce{NaCl}\)
  • \(\ce{C6H12O6}\)

We can also classify matter like this:

Pure Substances

Pure element (like \(\ce{O2}\)) or pure compound (like \(\ce{H2O}\))

  • Water (\(\ce{H2O}\))
  • Table Salt (\(\ce{NaCl}\))
  • Glucose (\(\ce{C6H12O6}\))

Mixtures

Made of two or more different types of non-bonded compounds or elements

  • Salt Water (\(\ce{H2O + NaCl}\))
  • \(\ce{C6H12O6 + NaCl}\)
  • \(\ce{C6H12O6 + H2O}\)

And mixtures can be further divided into the following subcategories:

Homogeneous Mixtures

Uniform composition throughout

  • Salt Water*
  • Air
  • Un-curdled milk

Heterogeneous Mixtures

Non-uniform composition

  • Oil and Water
  • Sand and Salt
  • Salad and pizza

*In the case of salt water, if you add too much salt it stops dissolving in the water and is thus Heterogeneous

Chemical Properties/Change

If a change changes one substance into another it is a chemical change

Chemical properties/change

  • Burning paper on fire: \(\ce{C6H12O6 -> CO2+H2O}\)
  • Combustion/Flammability

Physical Properties/Change

  • Tearing paper in half: \(\ce{C6H12O6 -> CO2+H2O}\)
  • Freezing/melting things

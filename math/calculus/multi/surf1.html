A derivation about the method you learned in calc 1 to find surface area!

\[\text{Remember that when you revolve a function }f(x)\text{ around the }y \text{ axis, the resulting surface area is:}\] \[\int_a^b{f(x)\sqrt{1+(f'(x))^2}dx}\] \[\text{We can use parametric equations to derive this:}\] \[\text{Forming circles of radius }f(x), \vec{r}= \begin{bsmallmatrix} x & f(x)\cos\theta & f(x)\sin\theta \end{bsmallmatrix}\] \] \[\] \[\text{Surface area for a parametric surface is: }\int_a^b\int_c^d{\mid\vec{r_u}\times\vec{r_v}\mid{du}{dv}}\]

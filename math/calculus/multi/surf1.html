A derivation about the method you learned in calc 1 to find surface area!

\[\text{Remember that when you revolve a function }f(x)\text{ around the }y \text{ axis, the resulting surface area is:}\] \[\int_a^b{2\pif(x)\sqrt{1+(f'(x))^2}dx}\] \[\text{We can use parametric equations to derive this:}\] \[\text{Forming circles of radius }f(x), \vec{r}(x, \theta)= \begin{bmatrix} x & f(x)\cos\theta & f(x)\sin\theta \end{bmatrix}\] \] \[\text{Surface area for a parametric equation is: }\int_a^b\int_c^d{\mid\vec{r_u}\times\vec{r_v}\mid{du}{dv}}\] \[\text{We can see that }\vec{r_x}= \begin{bmatrix} 1 & f'(x)\cos\theta & f'(x)\sin\theta \end{bmatrix} \] \[\text{and }\vec{r_\theta}= \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -f(x)\sin\theta & f(x)\cos\theta \end{bmatrix} \] \[\text{So }\vec{r_x}\times\vec{r_\theta}= \begin{vmatrix} \hat{i} & \hat{j} & \hat{k} \\ 1 & f'(x)\cos\theta & f'(x)\sin\theta \\ 0 & -f(x)\sin\theta & f(x)\cos\theta \end{vmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} f'(x)f(x) & -f(x)\cos\theta & -f(x)\sin\theta \end{bmatrix} \] \[\implies\mid\vec{r_x}\times\vec{r_\theta}\mid=\sqrt{f'(x)^2f(x)^2+f(x)^2\cos^2\theta+f(x)\sin^2\theta}=\sqrt{(1+f'(x)^2)f(x)^2}=f(x)\sqrt{1+f'(x)^2}\] \[\implies{S}=\int_0^{2\pi}\int_a^b{f(x)\sqrt{1+f'(x)^2}}dxd\theta=\int_a^b{2\pi{f(x)}\sqrt{1+f'(x)^2}}dx\]

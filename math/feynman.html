A new tecnique to find \int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}

\[\text{Let's first revisit the traditional way of computing the guassian integral: }\] \[\text{Let }I=\int_{-infty}^{infty}{e^{-x^2}}dx\] \[\implies{I^2}=\int_{-infty}^{infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}\cdot\int_{-infty}^{infty}{e^{-y^2}dy}\] \[I^2=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{-(x^2+y^2)}dydx\] \[I^2=\int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\infty}{e^{-r^2}rdrd\theta}=2\pi\cdot\frac{1}{2}\int_{-\infty}^{0}{e^udu}\] \[\impliesI=\int_{-infty}^{infty}{e^{-x^2}}dx=\sqrt{\pi}\]

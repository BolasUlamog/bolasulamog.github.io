A new tecnique to find \(\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}\)

\[\text{Let's first revisit the traditional way of computing the guassian integral: }\] \[\text{Let }I=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}}dx\] \[\implies{I^2}=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}\cdot\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-y^2}dy}\] \[I^2=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}e^{-(x^2+y^2)}dydx\] \[I^2=\int_{0}^{2\pi}\int_{0}^{\infty}{e^{-r^2}rdrd\theta}=2\pi\cdot\frac{1}{2}\int_{-\infty}^{0}{e^udu}\] \[\implies{I}=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}}dx=\sqrt{\pi}\] \[\] \[\text{Now computing the same integral using Feynman sub!!}\] \[\text{Consider just half of the integral: }I=\int_{0}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}\] \[\text{Let }I(t)=\int_{0}^{\infty}{\frac{e^{-x^2}}{1+(\frac{x}{t})^2}}\text{ s.t. }I(\infty)=\int_{0}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}\] \[\text{Now apply the substitution }u=\frac{x}{t}, t\cdot{du}=dx\implies{I(t)}=t\int_0^{\infty}{\frac{e^{-u^2t^2}}{1+u^2}}du\] \[\text{Multiply by }e^{-t^2}\text{...}e^{-t^2}\frac{I(t)}{t}=\int_0^{\infty}{\frac{e^{-t^2(1+x^2)}}{1+x^2}}dx\] \[\implies{\frac{d}{dt}}(t^{-1}e^{-t^2}I(t))=\int_0^{\infty}\frac{d}{dt}({\frac{e^{-t^2(1+x^2)}}{1+x^2}})dx=\int_0^{\infty}{-2te^{-t^2(1+x^2)}}dx\] \[=-2te^{-t^2}\int_0^{\infty}{e^{-t^2x^2}}dx=-2e^{-t^2}\int_0^{\infty}{e^{-u^2}}du=-2e^{-t^2}I(\infty)\] \[\implies{\int_0^{\infty}\frac{d}{dt}}(t^{-1}e^{-t^2}I(t))dt=\int_0^{\infty}-2e^{-t^2}I(\infty)dt=-2I(\infty)^2\] \[\text{Since the left hand is equal to }-\lim_{t\to 0}\frac{I(t)}{t}=-\lim_{t\to 0}{\int_0^{\infty}{\frac{e^{-u^2t^2}}{1+u^2}}du}=-\int_0^{\infty}{\frac{1}{1+u^2}}du=-\lim_{x \to \infty}\arctan{x}=-\frac{\pi}{4}\text{, }\] \[\text{This means }{-\frac{\pi}{4}}=-2I(\infty)^2\implies{I(\infty)=\frac{\sqrt\pi}{2}}\] \[\implies2I(\infty)=\int_{-\infty}^{\infty}{e^{-x^2}dx}=\sqrt\pi\]

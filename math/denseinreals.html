\[\text{Prove that the rational numbers are dense in the reals}\] \[\text{Let }S=(a,b)\] \[\text{We can assume} 1=n(b-a) \text{ for some }n \in \mathbb{N}\text{. (Archimedes)}\] \[\implies{b}-a>\frac{1}{n}\] \[\text{So }\{b-\frac{1}{n},a+\frac{1}{n}\}\subset{S}\] \[\text{Now consider some }p\in\mathbb{Z}\text{ in which } p\leq{n}a, n\in\mathbb{Z}\]
